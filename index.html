Event Overview

Asset Integrity, Maintenance, Corrosion & Turnaround Conference Norway 2026

The Norway Asset Integrity, Corrosion & Turnaround Conference 2026 is a dedicated technical platform focused on the integrity, corrosion, reliability, and turnaround challenges facing the Norwegian Continental Shelf.

Hosted in Stavanger, Norway's energy capital, the conference brings together operators, regulators, technical authorities, and engineering leaders responsible for some of Europe's most critical offshore and gas infrastructure.This invitation-only conference delivers a full day of technical learning, strategic discussion, and industry collaboration.

From mature offshore assets and gas export infrastructure to carbon storage networks and future energy systems, this is where critical engineering challenges are discussed and practical solutions are explored.

Strategic Challenges

THE 5 STRATEGIC CHALLENGES THE NORWAY ASSET INTEGRITY, CORROSION & TURNAROUND CONFERENCE 2026 WILL ADDRESS

01. Operating Offshore Assets Beyond Original Design Life

The Reality

Many Norwegian Continental Shelf assets were never expected to operate this long, creating new integrity, maintenance, and reliability challenges.

Key Concerns Include

  • Life extension strategies.
  • Equipment obsolescence.
  • Fitness-for-service assessments.

The Risk: Operators must balance asset longevity with safety, reliability, and operational performance.

02. Corrosion Under Insulation (CUI)

The Reality

CUI remains one of the most persistent and costly integrity threats across Norway's mature offshore and processing assets.

Key Concerns Include

  • Early detection.
  • Risk-based inspection.
  • Coating performance.

The Risk: Hidden corrosion can lead to unexpected failures, increased costs, and production disruptions.

Managing Integrity During Major Turnarounds

The Reality

Turnarounds are among the highest-risk periods for offshore facilities, requiring careful planning and execution.

Key Concerns Include

  • Scope growth.
  • Contractor productivity.
  • Workpack planning.

The Risk: Poor execution can result in delays, cost overruns, and increased integrity risk.

04. Protecting Critical Gas Infrastructure

The Reality

Norway's gas export infrastructure plays a vital role in European energy security and requires exceptional reliability.

Key Concerns Include

  • Pipeline integrity.
  • Compression systems.
  • Processing facilities.

The Risk: Infrastructure failures can impact production continuity and regional energy supply.

05. Infrastructure Readiness for CCS & Future Energy Systems

The Reality

Existing offshore infrastructure is being evaluated for future carbon capture and storage applications.

Key Concerns Include

  • CO₂ transport integrity.
  • Asset repurposing.
  • Long-term monitoring.

The Risk: New operating conditions introduce integrity challenges that require proactive management.

Why These 5 Matter

The operational pressure points shaping the future of the Norwegian Continental Shelf.
